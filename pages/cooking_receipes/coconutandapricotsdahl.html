Coconut and Apricots Dahl

I like Indian food a lot and if I had one red lentil based main course to recomand it will be this one. This receipe is an adaptation of a mango and coco dahl of the "Refugie Comunity Kitchen" 2019 Calendar.


Ingredients

- Butternut squash (1)
- Red Lentils (400 g)
- Onion (1)
- Garlic (4 cloves)
- Ginger (5 cm piece)
- Dry apricots (100 g)
- Coconut cream (250 g)
- Olive oil (150 ml)
- Vegetable stock (1.5 L)
- Cumin (3 tsp)
- Tumeric powder (2 tsp)
- Salt and peper for taste

Optionnal:
- Fresh coriander (small bunch)
- Seeds : cumin, coriander, sunfower and/or pumpkin (3 tsp each)

Instructions

For the butternut squash:
- Pre-heat the oven at 190 C.
- Cut the butternut squash in 1 cm cubes.
- In a bowl put the cubes and 50 ml of olive oil and mix, then let them absorb the oil during 20 minutes.
- Put the cubes on a baking tray and roat it in the oven 30 minutes.

For the dahl base:
- Cut finely the onion, the garlic and grind the ginger.
- In a large sauce pan, put 50 ml of olive oil and fry the garlic and the ginger at medium fire during 1 minute.
- You can also put some coriander and cumin seeds (3 tsp each) at this step.
- Add the onions and wait, until it begins to brown.
- Add the lentil, the coconut cream and the vegetable stock.
- Add cumin and tumeric powder.
- When it begins to boil, reduce the fire and cover.
- Let it cook for around 40 minutes (stir regularly to prenvent sticking).
- 5 minutes before the end, add the roasted butternut squash and the dried appricot (cut in 0.5 cm pieces).

For the coriander:
- Grind finely the fresh coriander with 50 ml of olive oil.
- Put the coriander oil on the dahl directly in your plate.

Notes

If like me, you like it more thick you can reduce the quantity of water (and vegetable stock) you use.
You can add sunflower or pumpkin seeds at the last moment to bring a little bit of crunch.
